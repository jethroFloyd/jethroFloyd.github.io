We live in a world

More than a thousand years ago

the weavers of ancient India perfected the art of spinning clothes out of cotton, in a purely organic manner...

But in the colonial era



the factories of Manchester replaced the traditional yarns. Thousands of cotton farmers gave up the trade and fled.

It was Mahatma Gandhi who took up Khadi as a symbol of Indian resistance, heritage and history.

But sadly, after that, Khadi has been neglected, in spite of being organic and comfortable. Traditional weavers could no longer sustain themselves. And Khadi nearly died an uneventful, silent death...

Until, of course we decided to step in...

... and decided to reinvent the way the world sees Khadi.
